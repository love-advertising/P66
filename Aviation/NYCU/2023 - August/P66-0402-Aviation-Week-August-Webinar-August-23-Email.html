Learning from a Mistake: When Aviation Fuel is Contaminated by Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF)
 
Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) contamination with Fuel System Icing Inhibitor (FSII) is a very serious threat to the aircraft fueling system. There have been multiple incidents where DEF was mistaken for FSII, leading to inflight emergencies, including both partial and complete engine failures.
 
During this live video learning session you will learn about the near tragic results from the breakdown or lack of procedures at an FBO:
 
  • What happens when DEF comes in contact with jet fuel
  • How DEF contaminated jet fuel in the aircraft
  • Ways to prevent DEF contamination from happening again
