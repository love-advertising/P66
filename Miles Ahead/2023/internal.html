 Overview of the Excellence in
 Action Program
 
Let's kick things off, Josh. Could you provide us with an overview of the Excellence in Action program and its primary objectives for station owners and operators?
 
JOSH: Absolutely, The Excellence in Action program is all about helping station owners and operators enhance the customer experience at their site(s). Its primary goals encompass evaluating and improving various facets of the customer journey, which includes aspects like cleanliness, service quality, and adherence to brand standards. Through the execution of mystery shops and the collection of valuable feedback, the program strives to elevate customer satisfaction levels, foster loyalty, and ultimately enhance overall business performance.
 
 Why the Change to IPSOS?
 
Now, the decision to revise the Excellence in Action program shop and partner with IPSOS – what prompted this shift, and what benefits can it help provide?
 
JOSH: Well, we recognize that in today’s competitive landscape, it’s more critical than ever for sites to deliver an exceptional customer experience. The decision to modernize and optimize the mystery shopping process reflects our commitment to delivering the best possible customer experience. As we evaluated the program, we found that IPSOS offered robust solutions that were unmatched. IPSOS is the largest Mystery Shopping provider globally and brings a wealth of experience and expertise. IPSOS not only offers elevated shopper experiences but also aligns seamlessly with our aspiration to maintain the highest standards in assessing and enhancing our customer experience.
 
What set IPSOS apart from other Mystery Shop providers?
 
JOSH: IPSOS provides the ability to provide a comprehensive view of the customer journey, encompassing both physical and digital touchpoints. This holistic approach enables our team to thoroughly analyze the data collected and integrate it with other research insights from across the company. The result? We have a better understanding of what drives consumer loyalty and preference for each of our fuel brands.
 
Additionally, the technology platform provided by IPSOS offers a seamless and user-friendly experience for data collection, real-time feedback, and best-in-class reporting. In essence, this transition to IPSOS brings numerous benefits, not only to Phillips 66 but, more importantly, to our customers. It solidifies our dedication to staying current with industry best practices and ensures that the Excellence in Action program remains highly effective in delivering valuable insights that ultimately enrich our customers' experiences.
 
 Beyond Finding Faults
 
Many people seem to think that mystery shop programs are solely about catching mistakes and pointing out faults. Could you elaborate on why these programs are incredibly valuable for gas station owners and operators beyond just finding errors?
 
JOSH: Absolutely, that's an excellent point to clarify. While it might appear that these programs primarily focus on spotting mistakes, their scope goes far beyond that perception. In reality, they offer an invaluable perspective that can be challenging to attain through internal assessments alone.
 
Consider this scenario: You're running a gas station or convenience store day in and day out. You become intimately familiar with every aspect of your operation. However, this deep familiarity can sometimes lead to unintentional blind spots. This is precisely where the value of mystery shopping comes into play.
 
Our mystery shopping program isn't limited to merely pointing out minor faults or nitpicking details. It's firmly grounded in extensive consumer market research and designed to extract insights that often go unspoken. Let's take the example of a taped-up handwritten note on a dispenser that reads "out of order." At first glance, it might seem inconsequential, and some of our own customers may share that sentiment. However, our program takes a deeper dive.
 
We understand that seemingly minor details can serve as indicators of more significant issues. That handwritten note might signify something more profound than the inconvenience of a non-functional dispenser; for a consumer, it may trigger concerns about the payment security of the dispenser. By identifying such cues and patterns, we assist gas station owners and operators in addressing potential issues before they escalate. Mystery shopping provides an objective lens through which owners and operators can view their sites, enabling them to gain a more profound understanding of their customers' unspoken needs and expectations. In essence, it's not solely about catching mistakes; it's about fostering continuous improvement and delivering the best possible experience for every consumer.
 
 How Does Mystery Shopping
 Actually Work
 
Could you break down how the mystery shopping process functions within the program and highlight the benefits it offers to station owners and operators?
 
JOSH: Certainly. The mystery shopping process entails sending trained evaluators to sites as anonymous customers. These evaluators comprehensively assess various facets of the customer experience, encompassing elements such as cleanliness, staff friendliness, and adherence to brand standards. The findings they gather provide station owners and operators with objective insights into their operations. These insights, in turn, help identify specific areas for improvement, enhance staff training, and ultimately lead to a superior customer experience.
 
 
  The New Guidebook
 
Tell us more about the brand-new Excellence in Action Guidebook and its role in aiding our customers in comprehending and improving their site performance.
 
JOSH: The Excellence in Action Guidebook is an exciting addition to our toolkit, and we're thrilled to introduce it. This guidebook serves as a valuable resource, simplifying the mystery shopping process by removing guesswork and ambiguity.
 
Within its pages, you'll discover clear visuals that distinctly outline what constitutes a correct or compliant action, as well as what falls into the non-compliant category. Moreover, the guidebook delves even deeper by providing associated grading criteria. Essentially, it serves as a detailed roadmap that empowers teams to gain a precise understanding of what mystery shoppers evaluate during their visits.
 
By leveraging the Excellence in Action Guidebook, our customers can effectively align their employee teams with program expectations. It proves instrumental in identifying areas for improvement, elevating customer service standards, and ensuring robust compliance with brand standards. Armed with this guidebook, teams can confidently take proactive steps to enhance their site's performance and provide an elevated experience to their customers.

Anticipating shifts in customer expectations is vital. How do you envision customer expectations at gas stations evolving in the coming years, and how is the Excellence in Action program equipped to meet these changes?
 
JOSH: Indeed, staying ahead of evolving consumer expectations is paramount. In the forthcoming years, we anticipate consumers to place even greater emphasis on factors like convenience, sustainability, and the seamless integration of technologies like the ones Chris Gilliland has shared with our readers previously.

 Preparing for Mystery Shops
 
What strategies or best practices would you recommend for station owners and operators to prepare for and effectively respond to mystery shop assessments?
 
JOSH: To prepare effectively, there are several steps to consider. First and foremost, immersing oneself in the new guidebook is essential. It's a treasure trove of insights!
 
Beyond that, staff education is paramount. It's imperative to ensure that all staff members are well-versed in the mystery shopping program and its objectives. This heightened awareness can positively contribute to various aspects of the assessment, like customer greetings.
 
Consistency is another vital factor. Maintain consistent service and cleanliness standards across your site. This consistency not only enhances the overall customer experience but also reflects positively when you encounter mystery shop evaluations.
 
Regularly reviewing mystery shop results through the IPSOS dashboard is an essential practice. This approach allows you to gain a comprehensive understanding of your performance and pinpoint specific areas requiring improvement. These results serve as invaluable feedback. To maximize their potential, consider providing ongoing training to your staff, which should be tailored based on the feedback and recommendations from mystery shops. This empowers your team to continuously enhance their performance, delivering exceptional service to customers consistently.
 
 
  Challenges and Misconceptions
 
In your experience, what are some common challenges or misconceptions people have when participating in a mystery shopping program like Excellence in Action?
 
JOSH: Certainly, in my experience, some common challenges and misconceptions tend to surface. One challenge involves resistance to feedback; individuals or teams may sometimes feel threatened by negative evaluations, inadvertently missing out on the opportunity for valuable improvement. Additionally, there's a tendency for some to view mystery shopping as a one-time effort. In reality, it should be perceived as an ongoing process for continuous improvement. This perspective is essential as it enables the site to consistently provide exceptional experiences to its customers, reinforcing the importance of embracing mystery shopping as a valuable tool for growth and excellence.
 
 
 SUCCESS STORIES
 
Could you share any success stories or examples of how our customers' businesses have benefited from the Excellence in Action program?
 
JOSH: Certainly, we've witnessed numerous instances where sites have significantly improved their scores through the program's cure options. Before the Excellence in Action Program, there were no opportunities for cures, which meant that everything had to be perfect on the day of the assessment. Today, we provide sites with the chance to address and cure certain items, which has led to a noticeable increase in scores across our network. This approach fosters a more collaborative atmosphere, focusing on improvement rather than punitive measures.
