Can you talk through a few of the key trends that you've seen over the past few months?

Chris: Certainly. Each day brings new headlines, and it's hard to predict where the story will ultimately lead. However, there's no denying that significant changes are taking place in the electric charging industry. In the last 24 months, one notable trend has been the introduction of various incentives by the federal government. These incentives aim to promote the development of public ultra-fast charging infrastructure and encourage consumers to purchase electric vehicles. For example, the Build Back America Act has allocated $5 billion over five years to be distributed across all 50 states. Additionally, the Inflation Recovery Act provides a tax credit of up to $7,500 for electric vehicle purchases. These measures work in tandem to create a conducive environment for the transition towards electric vehicles.
 
With these incentives in place, consumers have more confidence and motivation to embrace electric vehicles. However, we understand that there are still some concerns and frustrations among consumers. What are the main issues they currently face, and how are we addressing them?

Ryan: The three primary concerns we hear from consumers are range anxiety, equipment reliability, and the time required for charging. Range anxiety refers to the fear of running out of battery power while driving. To alleviate this concern, we are working on expanding our network of branded sites to increase the availability of charging stations. As for equipment reliability, we have learned valuable lessons from those who have come before us. We leverage their experiences to ensure our charging stations meet the highest standards. The federal and state governments have set reliability targets of 97%, and we strive to not only meet but exceed these benchmarks. Lastly, charging time is a crucial factor. Our ultra-fast chargers can provide a 100-mile range in approximately 10 to 15 minutes, significantly reducing the time required for charging.

When we talk about ultra-fast charging, we're aiming for power levels of 150 kilowatts or even higher. This focus allows us to provide a significantly faster charging experience for electric vehicle owners. Our goal is to offer a convenience factor that can rival the time it takes to fill up a traditional gas-powered vehicle. By prioritizing ultra-fast charging, we believe we can alleviate one of the main concerns potential electric vehicle buyers have, which is range anxiety.Range anxiety poses a real barrier to widespread electric vehicle adoption. People worry about running out of charge and not having a convenient way to recharge their vehicles. By offering ultra-fast charging, we can address this concern and give people the confidence to make the switch to electric vehicles.

Chris:  It's not just about the convenience for consumers, though. Ultra-fast charging also presents opportunities for businesses. With faster charging times, electric vehicle owners are more inclined to visit establishments that provide these charging stations. It becomes an added incentive for customers to choose one establishment over another. So, it's a win-win situation for both consumers and businesses.

Ryan: Indeed. As we mentioned earlier, partnerships play a crucial role in overcoming some of the challenges in this business. We actively collaborate with charging infrastructure providers, automakers, and other stakeholders to build a robust charging network. This network will not only benefit our customers but also contribute to the overall growth of the electric vehicle ecosystem.

Now, let's delve into the economics of running an EV charging business. There seems to be a common misconception that EV charging is not a profitable endeavor or, at best, a low-margin one. Could you shed some light on the economics and profitability of running a charger at a site?

Chris: The landscape has evolved, and it's important to dispel the notion that running EV charging is unprofitable. While it's not a guaranteed success, it is definitely possible to operate an EV charging business profitably. One of the key factors influencing profitability is the utilization rate of the charging stations. Higher utilization means more revenue generation. Implementing pricing structures that account for different charging speeds or offering subscription plans for regular users can also contribute to profitability.
 
Are there any specific challenges or considerations that need to be addressed to ensure profitability?

Ryan: Absolutely. One of the significant challenges is the upfront investment required for installing charging infrastructure, especially for ultra-fast chargers. However, there are government incentives and grants available to offset these costs, making it financially viable. Managing electricity costs and ensuring a reliable power supply are also crucial factors. By negotiating favorable electricity rates and exploring renewable energy options, operational costs can be optimized. Additionally, ongoing maintenance and support for the charging equipment should be considered. Regular maintenance, software updates, and reliable service providers are essential for ensuring smooth operations and maximizing profitability.

If a customer is interested in installing a charger at their site, what criteria should they consider?

Ryan: Before making an investment decision, there are a few key considerations for customers. Firstly, they need to assess whether they have sufficient space to accommodate the charger and dedicate parking spaces for EV charging. Secondly, exploring grant opportunities at the state or federal level is crucial. We are here to assist our customers in accessing available grant programs. Finally, the location of the site is a vital factor to consider. Areas with higher projected EV adoption rates, such as the West Coast or Colorado, present more favorable conditions for installing chargers.
 
I'd like to discuss the new customer program that will be rolling out soon. How do Conoco and 76 branded chargers fit into this program?

Ryan: We are excited to announce that we can now offer our branded customers discounted FreeWire charging units. These co-branded units, featuring the Conoco®, 76®, and Phillips 66® logos, are visually appealing and aligned with our brand images. This initial offering is just the beginning, as we plan to expand the range of available equipment in the future. Our goal is to provide the right equipment that matches each site's specific needs. We believe this program is a significant step forward, and we are eager to bring it to our customers.
